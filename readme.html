Map the Gap NYC

An interactive tool showing the gap between New York City’s official “affordable housing” income benchmarks and what households in each neighborhood actually earn.

Live site: https://theospohngellert.github.io/map-the-gap-nyc/

What it shows

NYC prices affordable housing against a regional Area Median Income (AMI) — $152,700 for a 3-person household in 2026 — calculated across the entire metro region, including its wealthiest suburban counties. This tool compares that benchmark against the real median household income in all 55 Furman Center sub-borough areas covering the entire city, showing:

  • The gap map — every neighborhood’s median income as a percentage of regional AMI
  • Gap cards — what rent each neighborhood’s median household can actually afford (30%-of-income standard) vs. what “affordable” units at 60%, 80%, and 130% AMI charge
  • A personal calculator — enter household income and size to see your real AMI percentage, where you sit on NYC’s AMI scale, which lottery bands you’d qualify for, and what rent is genuinely affordable to you

Data sources

  • Neighborhood incomes: NYU Furman Center CoreData.nyc, median household income by sub-borough area, in 2025 dollars (latest available year per area: 2024; one area uses 2023)
  • AMI: NYC HPD Area Median Income schedule, HUD FY2026
  • “Affordable” rent formula: 30% of the band’s income ceiling ÷ 12

Full methodology and caveats are in the Method & Sources section of the site.

Data as of: July 2026. Income data refreshes annually with new ACS releases; AMI updates each spring.
