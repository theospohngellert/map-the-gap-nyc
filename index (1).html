Map the Gap NYC

NYC prices its "affordable housing" against a regional Area Median Income of $152,700 (100% AMI, 3-person household, 2026) — a figure computed across the whole metro region, Westchester included. The median household in Mott Haven earns $27,364. This tool shows the gap for every neighborhood in the city, and what it means for your rent.

Method & sources

AMI: HUD FY2026 for the NY metro region, as published by NYC HPD: $152,700 = 100% AMI for a 3-person household. Other household sizes estimated with standard HUD ratios (70/80/90/100/108/116% of the 4-person base). Verify exact figures against HPD's official chart before relying on them.

Neighborhood incomes: NYU Furman Center CoreData.nyc, median household income by sub-borough area, in 2025 dollars. Latest available year per area (2024; one area uses 2023). Neighborhood medians reflect households of all sizes; the regional comparison uses HUD's 3-person figure, the closest match to NYC's average household size of ~2.5. Sub-borough areas approximate the city's 59 community districts; a few low-population districts are combined (e.g. Mott Haven/Hunts Point). All 55 areas — the full city — are included.

"Affordable" rent: the standard formula — 30% of the band's income ceiling, divided by 12. Actual HPD rent charts vary slightly by unit size and utility allowances.

What this prototype isn't yet: live Housing Connect listings, a ZIP-code-level view, or annual auto-refresh. The Census-API pipeline for those is written and ready to run.
